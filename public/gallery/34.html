“This help keeps on going!”

Forest Cormelle, a young Haitian mother, would love to share her opinion on the Shared Accountability and Lending (SALT) microfinance program. Forest lives in the mountains of southern Haiti and was trapped in the cycle of poverty and despair for years. She knows how it feels to send children to bed without food, have no money for shoes, and be unable to afford school tuition. But for the past two years, Forest has been part of CAM’s new microfinance program and is excited about the changes taking place in her family.

A small loan from CAM’s SALT microfinance program enabled Forest to expand her little business of selling rice, oil, and flour in the local markets. As a result of the loan and teaching she received through SALT microfinance, the profit from her small business increased four hundred percent!

“For the first time,” Forest says, “we can buy shoes for the children, send them to school, and save a little each month for emergencies.”

Forest leaves her home at 5:00 a.m. to hike several hours over rugged mountain paths to the markets, while her husband tries to farm the steep hillside behind their small house. Making a living is extremely difficult, but Forest is excited and loves to share how the SALT microfinance program has blessed her family. “This is so much better for us than people giving us food,” she said enthusiastically. “When the food is gone, this help keeps on going!”
